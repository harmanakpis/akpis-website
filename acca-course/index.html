ACCA Syllabus

The ACCA syllabus is an extensive and demanding program that covers finance, business, and accounting aspects. The curriculum is divided into 3 levels:

  • Applied Knowledge Level
  • Applied Skills Level
  • Strategic Professional Level

ACCA Applied Knowledge Level

With the use of applied knowledge, you may better comprehend the function of accounting in a company setting and acquire the useful abilities and methods required of an accountant in the field. The subjects in ACCA Level 1 are:

Business Technology (BT)
The purpose of the ACCA paper on Business and Technology is to develop an understanding of the contemporary business environment, the operation of the accounting and finance teams in companies, and the fundamentals of effective management.
Financial Accounting (FA)
The ACCA paper on Financial Accounting begins with an overview of financial statements, profit and loss, laws of financial accounting, cash flow, and many more.
Management Accounting (MA)
ACCA paper on Management Accounting focuses on management decision-making processes, an analysis of quantitative information, and preparing multiple financial reports for a business.

Applied Skills Level

The skill level consists of advanced knowledge and strategic management skills. It consists of 6 ACCA Subjects:

Corporate and Business Law (LW)
Corporate and business law helps build your knowledge of the legal system in the business world. The course delves into the laws related to business, companies, financial affairs, business finance, and company regulation.
Performance Management (PM)
This is the next level of management accounting you studied in your knowledge level. In addition to using financial information for planning, decision-making, performance evaluation, and other purposes, it also incorporates both quantitative and qualitative data.
Taxation (TX)
In this course, you will learn about the fundamental concepts and laws related to taxation and an overview of income taxation for businesses and individuals. Furthermore, it will help me gain an understanding of value-added tax (VAT) and other indirect taxes and further explore tax-efficient strategies.
Financial Reporting (FR)
An extension of the financial accounting course, the ACCA paper on financial reporting aims to improve students' abilities in financial statement analysis and interpretation as well as how to put the accounts together in the form of financial reports.
Financial Management (FM)
The focus of the ACCA paper on financial management is on how an organisation manages its finances. Financial management teaches you about a company's whole financial life cycle, from raising capital to making profits to valuing the enterprise.
Assurance and Audit (AA)
The most important topic for chartered accountancy is audit, which is covered in the ACCA subject of Assurance and Audit. You get knowledge of the audit procedure, specifications, rules, and how every organisation must conduct an audit.

Strategic Professional Level

The Professional Level has been divided again into two segments. The candidates must choose two subjects from the optional section, although the exams in the Essential section are always required.

Strategic Business Leader (SBL)
To build governance in organisational management, SBL is the ideal paper. It consists of an understanding of ethical framework, Business strategies and corporate governance, risk assessment, and many more.
Strategic Business Reporting (SBR)
Gain an insight into the role of an accountant and how to maintain ethics and professionalism in the business. Furthermore, it comprises financial statement preparation and work on case studies to develop an understanding of more complex financial reporting issues.
Advanced Financial Management (AFM)
Develops your expertise as a senior finance professional with an in-depth understanding of financial markets and industry structures, integrating elements of business economics and finance.
Advanced Performance Management (APM)
It helps you gain the skills required to measure performance, optimise strategic development, and integrate economic conditions and external aspects.
Advanced Taxation (ATX)
The complexities of taxation are clarified by Advanced Taxation (ATX). Learn about commercial and international taxation, as well as how to efficiently handle tax matters while abiding by legal regulations.
Advanced Audit and Assurance(AAA)
The main goal of the Advanced Audit and Assurance(AAA) is to improve your audit and assurance abilities. Also, acquire the capacity to evaluate and guarantee the accuracy of risk management procedures, corporate governance, and financial data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ACCA certification?
The Association of Chartered Certified Accountants provides the internationally recognised ACCA course in the accounting and finance domains. The certification programme teaches students company management techniques in addition to accounting and finance knowledge. Candidates holding the ACCA qualification share a greater comprehension of accounting and finance, in addition to professional abilities, including decision-making and communication.
What is the average salary of an ACCA?
After completing the ACCA course, a candidate's pay is determined by a number of factors, such as their experience, location, talents, and corporate reputation. An ACCA-certified candidate is eligible to get an initial compensation package of Rs. 5 lacs annually. Also, an applicant with an ACCA certification may begin earning between 5 to 12 Lakhs annually, depending on a variety of factors.
How many exams can a student take at each exam session?
Students are permitted to take up to four exams in each exam cycle, for a total of up to eight different tests in a year. These tests may consist of any mix of computer-based and paper-based assessments.
Can all students who qualify 10+2 join ACCA?
ACCA certification is available to students who achieved 65% in English and Maths and 50% in the remaining disciplines of their 10+2 education. Through the foundation in Accountancy Route, which is an ACCA entry-level qualification, other students can become members of the ACCA. Students who passed their 12th grade exam but had a score below 65% only need to take the FBT, FMA, and FFA Foundation level exams.
